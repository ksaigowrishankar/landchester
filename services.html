What is Geotechnical Investigation?

Geotechnical investigation involves the study and analysis of soil, rock, and groundwater conditions at a construction site. It is a crucial step in the design and construction process, as it provides valuable insights into the site’s geological and geotechnical characteristics. By thoroughly evaluating the subsurface conditions, we can identify potential risks, assess the suitability of the site for construction, and recommend appropriate foundation design and ground improvement measures.

Field Testing

Plate Load Test: Assessing Soil Strength

Plate load testing is a geotechnical investigation method used to determine the load-bearing capacity and settlement characteristics of soil. By applying controlled loads to a steel plate on the ground surface and measuring the resulting settlement, engineers can assess the soil’s stiffness, modulus of subgrade reaction, and ultimate bearing capacity. This cost-effective and versatile test provides accurate data for foundation design and construction decision-making. Our experienced team conducts plate load testing using advanced equipment, providing reliable analysis and recommendations to ensure the stability and success of your projects. Contact us for professional plate load testing services.

Pile load test

Pile load testing involves applying controlled loads to a deep foundation pile and measuring the pile’s response to evaluate its load-carrying capacity. It helps determine the pile’s behavior under different load conditions, such as axial compression, axial tension, or lateral loads. By conducting pile load tests, engineers can validate design assumptions, optimize pile design, and ensure compliance with safety regulations.
